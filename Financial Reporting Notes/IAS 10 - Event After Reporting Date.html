IAS 10 - Event After Reporting Date

🎯

Objective:

IAS 10 addresses how to handle events that occur between:

  • The end of the reporting period (e.g., 31 Dec 20X1), and
  • The date the financial statements are authorized for issue (e.g., 31 March 20X2)

🔍 1. Types of Events

Event TypeDefinitionAccounting Treatment
✅Adjusting EventEvidence of conditions that existed at the reporting dateAdjust financial statements
❌Non-Adjusting EventEvidence of conditions that arose after the reporting dateDo NOT adjust, but disclose if material

✅ Examples of Adjusting Events:

  • Bankruptcy of a customer confirming an existing impairment
  • Settlement of a lawsuit confirming obligation at year-end
  • Inventory valuation error discovered with evidence that existed at year-end
  • Fraud or errors discovered from pre-year-end actions

❌ Examples of Non-Adjusting Events:

  • Major fire or flood after year-end
  • Decline in market value of investments after year-end
  • Issue of shares or dividends declared after year-end
  • Acquisition or disposal of a business after year-end

➡️ If material, these events must be disclosed in the notes.

🧾 2. Dividends

Dividends declared after the reporting period must not be recognized as a liability at year-end.

➡️ These should be disclosed only in the notes.

📅 3. Date of Authorization

The cut-off for events occurs when the financial statements are authorized for issue.

This date must be disclosed in the notes to the financial statements.

⚠️ 4. Going Concern Consideration

If events after the reporting period indicate that the company is no longer a going concern, do not prepare accounts on a going concern basis.

➡️ Instead, use break-up basis (e.g., liquidation values)

Exam Tips

Question StyleWhat You Must Do
“Identify” an adjusting or non-adjusting eventLook for whether the condition existed at year-end
“Explain” why something is or isn’t adjustedTie it to the evidence of conditions at reporting date
“State” the treatment of dividendsIf declared after year-end → Disclosure only
“Discuss” impact on going concernMention liquidation basis if business is no longer viable
