IAS 41 - Agriculture

IAS 41 applies to:

  • Biological assets (living animals and plants)
  • Agricultural produce (harvested products from biological assets)
  • Government grants related to biological assets

It does not apply to:

  • Land used for agriculture → IAS 16
  • Intangible agricultural rights (e.g., quotas) → IAS 38

Definitions

TermDefinition
Biological AssetA living plant or animal
Agricultural ProduceHarvested product (e.g., wool, milk, picked grapes)
Bearer PlantUsed to produce agricultural produce over several periods but not itself sold
HarvestDetachment from biological asset (e.g., milking, picking fruit)
Fair Value (FV)Selling price in an orderly market minus point-of-sale costs

Recognition Criteria (Like PPE)

A biological asset or produce is recognized when:

✅ Entity controls the asset

✅ Probable future economic benefits

✅ Fair value or cost can be reliably measured

Measurement Rules

Biological assets are measured at Fair value less point-of-sale costs (FVLCTS)

When FV cannot be measured reliably, use the Cost model.

Subsequent Measurement:

  • Fair value model is the default approach
  • Record gains/losses from FV changes in Profit or Loss
  • FV must be remeasured annually
✅ Biological assets are not depreciated since they grow naturally.


Measurement Model

ItemMeasurement
Biological assets✅ Fair Value less Costs to Sell (FVLCTS)
Agricultural produce (at harvest)✅ FVLCTS
Bearer plants (e.g., fruit tree)❌ IAS 41 doesn’t apply → Use IAS 16
  • Re-measure at each reporting date at fair value less costs to sell
  • Changes go to P&L immediately

Bearer Plants Exception:

  • Fall under IAS 16 (PPE), not IAS 41
  • E.g., grapevines, tea bushes, rubber trees
  • Measured at cost model (and depreciated)

Agricultural Produce at Harvest

  • Measured at FV less costs to sell at harvest
  • Then becomes Inventory under IAS 2
  • Any gain/loss → goes to Profit or Loss

Government Grants under IAS 41

Grant TypeTreatment
Unconditional (e.g. per hectare owned)Recognize when receivable
Conditional (e.g. reforest area, grow trees)Recognize when conditions are met

Example: Dairy Cattle

  • Cows = biological asset
  • Milk = agricultural produce

At year-end:

  • Cows are measured at FVLCTS (market value minus selling costs)
  • Any change in fair value = gain/loss to P&L
  • Milk harvested is measured at FVLCTS, then becomes inventory under IAS 2
