IAS 37 - Provision , Contingent Asstes and Liabilities


1. Definitions

TermMeaning
ProvisionA liability of uncertain timing or amount (you expect to pay, but don’t know exactly how much or when)
Contingent LiabilityA possible obligation, or a present obligation that is not probable or cannot be reliably measured
Contingent AssetA possible asset that arises from past events, but will be confirmed by future events not in the entity’s control

2. Recognition Criteria for a Provision

A provision should be recognized only if ALL 3 conditions are met:

ConditionMeaning
🔸 Present obligationLegal or constructive obligation from past events
🔸 Probable outflowMore likely than not (>50%)
🔸 Reliable estimateAmount can be reasonably estimated

3 Measurement

  • Best estimate of the expenditure required to settle the obligation
  • Use present value if material (discounting is required)
  • Consider risks and uncertainties
  • Do not recognize gains from reimbursement assets until virtually certain

4. Common examples of Provisions

SituationProvision?
Legal case likely to lose✅ Yes, recognize provision
Warranty obligation✅ Yes, recognize provision
Redundancy announced with no formal plan❌ No (no present obligation)
Clean-up required by law✅ If legal obligation exists
Onerous contract (unavoidable loss)✅ Yes (least net cost of exit or completion)

5. Contingent Liabilities

Do not recognize in the statement of financial position
➡️ Only disclose in the notes unless
  • It is remote → No disclosure needed

6. Contingent Assets

Never recognize a contingent asset.
➡️ Only disclose in the notes if inflow is probable
➡️ Recognize only when inflow is virtually certain > becomes an asset

7. Disclosures Required

  • Movement in provision (opening balance, additions, used, reversed)
  • Nature of obligation
  • Expected timing and uncertainty
  • Reimbursement (if any)
