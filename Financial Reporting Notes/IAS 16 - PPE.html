IAS 16 - PPE

To prescribe how to recognize, measure, and depreciate tangible non-current assets such as buildings, machinery, and vehicles.

PPE are:

  • Tangible Assets
  • Held for use in:
    • Production of goods/services
    • Rental to others
    • Administrative purposes

Recognize Criteria

PPE item is recognized as an asset when:

  1. Probable future economic benefits will flow to the entity
  1. Cost of the item can be measured reliably

📌 If not — treat as an expense

Initial Measurement : at cost

Cost Includes:

  • Purchase price (less trade discounts)
  • Non-refundable import duties and taxes
  • Delivery, handling, installation, and site preparation costs
  • Professional fees (such as engineers and architects)
  • Testing costs (net of revenue from test production)
  • Dismantling and restoration costs (present value of future obligations)

Excluded Costs:

Charged to P&L:

  • Admin & general overheads
  • Initial operating losses
  • Relocation or training
  • Repairs or maintenance

Expenditure TypeTreatment
Repairs, maintenance❌ Expense
Enhancements, overhaul✅ Capitalize (if increases future benefit)
Major inspection✅ Capitalize and depreciate

Measurement Models

ModelDescription
Cost modelCarrying amount = Cost − Accumulated Depreciation − Impairment
Revaluation modelRevalue to fair value (if reliably measurable) − Accumulated Depreciation/Impairment

📌 Revaluation must be done for entire asset class, not just individual items.

Method of Depreciation

Straight Line: (Cost - Residual Value) ÷ Useful Life

Reducing Balance: Carrying Amount x Reducing Rate

Change in Depreciation Method/ Useful Life

  • Apply prospectively (current + future years only)
