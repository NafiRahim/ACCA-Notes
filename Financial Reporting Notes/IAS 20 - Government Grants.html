IAS 20 - Government Grants

Objective

To prescribe accounting treatment for:

  1. Government grants (cash, free assets, etc.)
  1. Government assistance (non-monetary aid, like licenses or free services)

TermMeaning
Government GrantA transfer of resources from government to an entity in return for compliance with conditions
Government AssistanceGovernment action that benefits a business (e.g. tax relief, free advice) but cannot be reliably measured

➡️ Only grants are accounted under IAS 20.

➡️ Assistance is only disclosed — not recognized in financial statements.

Recognition Conditions for Grants

Recognize only when BOTH are met:

  1. Reasonable assurance the entity will comply with grant conditions
  1. Grant will be received

📌 Do not recognize when:

  • Conditions are unclear
  • Repayment is likely

Types of Grants and Their Treatment

TypeDescriptionTreatment
Grant related to incomeHelps cover expenses or losses, or supports a businessRecognized in P&L over the periods in which the costs arise
Grant related to assetHelps purchase or build an assetTwo options: 1️⃣ Deduct from asset’s carrying amount (net method) 2️⃣ Show as deferred income and amortize to P&L over asset’s life (gross method)
